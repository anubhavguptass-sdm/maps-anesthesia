Video Laryngoscope:

In the event that an advanced technique for airway visualization needs to be employed there will be an additional charge. A video laryngoscope is usually needed in a small subpopulation of patients that have difficult airways secondary to

1. Syndromes that affect the face and neck

2. Body habitus that are associated with redundant tissue in the airway and short/thick necks

3. Any condition that causes trouble with night time breathing and manifests as loud snoring and moments of apnea and usually necessitate the use of a CPAP to maintain airway patency.

Urinary catheter

There will be an additional charge In the event that the anesthesiologist deems it necassary that a urinary catheter is essential in order to provide a safe anesthetic. In short surgeries that are not longer than 3 hours, there is usually no effect on the bladder. The bladder will become distended in longer cases and the patient could become incontinent over time. For this purpose, a foley catheter is typically placed prior to surgery and keeps the bladder empty throughout.

BIS Monitor

There will be an additional charge in the event the anesthesiologist decides that a brain activity monitor (BIS) would improve your care. A bispectral index (BIS) monitor is commonly utilized to assess depth of sedation when administering sedative, hypnotic, or anesthetic agents during surgical and medical procedures. The depth of sedation is calculated by measuring cerebral electrical activity via an electroencephalogram (EEG)
